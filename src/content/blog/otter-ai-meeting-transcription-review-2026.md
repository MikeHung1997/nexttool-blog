## The Real Cost of Manual Note-Taking

Most people underestimate how much cognitive load goes into taking notes during a meeting. You are simultaneously listening, processing, deciding what matters, and writing — all while trying to contribute to the conversation. Something always gets dropped.

The downstream cost is real: missed follow-ups, repeated discussions, decisions that no one can verify, and time spent after the meeting reconstructing what happened from memory.

For teams that run on async communication, remote work, or frequent client calls, this friction compounds fast.

## What Otter.ai Actually Does

Otter.ai joins your meetings as an AI participant — on Zoom, Google Meet, or Microsoft Teams — and does the following in real time:

- **Transcribes the conversation** with speaker identification, so you can see who said what
- **Generates a summary** at the end of the meeting, highlighting key points and action items
- **Allows search** across your transcripts, so you can find any moment from any past meeting

- **Lets participants add comments and highlights** during the meeting itself
- **Integrates with tools** like Salesforce, HubSpot, Slack, and Notion

The transcription is not perfect — proper nouns, heavy accents, and technical jargon can trip it up — but for standard business conversation it is accurate enough to be genuinely useful. The summaries are the standout feature for most users: a concise overview of what was discussed and what needs to happen next, delivered without any manual effort.

## Who Should Look Elsewhere

Otter.ai is not the right fit for everyone.

If you primarily need transcription for recorded video or audio files rather than live meetings, tools like Descript or Whisper-based alternatives may serve you better. Otter's strength is the live meeting experience — the integration with calendar and video conferencing, the real-time summaries, the collaborative layer.

If you are working in a language other than English, check the current language support carefully. Otter's accuracy is strongest in English.

And if your primary pain point is organizing notes and knowledge rather than capturing conversations, a tool like Notion AI may address more of your workflow.
