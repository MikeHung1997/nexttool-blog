Most AI writing tools want your credit card before you can see if they're actually useful. These ones don't.

Here are the best free AI writing tools in 2026 — tested, ranked, and sorted by who they're actually for.

## Free Pick: Grammarly

Grammarly's free plan catches grammar and spelling errors, suggests clearer phrasing, and works directly in your browser, Google Docs, and most writing apps. It's not an AI content generator — it's an AI editor, which is actually more useful for most people.

**Best for:** Anyone who writes regularly and wants a second pair of eyes.  
**Free plan limits:** Basic grammar and spelling checks. Tone and clarity suggestions require the paid plan.  
[Try Grammarly free →](https://grammarly.com)

## Pro Pick: ChatGPT

ChatGPT's free tier gives you access to GPT-4o with usage limits. For drafting, brainstorming, rewriting, and research assistance, it's still the most versatile free AI writing tool available.

**Best for:** Content creators, students, and anyone who needs a general-purpose writing assistant.  
**Free plan limits:** Usage caps during peak hours. No memory across conversations on the free plan.  
[Try ChatGPT free →](https://chat.openai.com)

## Beginner Pick: Notion AI

If you already use Notion for notes or project management, the built-in AI features are worth trying. You get 20 free AI responses per month before needing to upgrade.

**Best for:** Notion users who want AI assistance without switching apps.  
**Free plan limits:** 20 responses per month.  
[Try Notion AI free →](https://notion.so)

## The bottom line

For most people, the combination of Grammarly (for editing) and ChatGPT (for drafting) covers 90% of writing needs without spending anything. Start there before paying for anything else.
